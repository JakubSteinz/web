The Long Road Home

I. The Fall of Farewell

It started with a virus. Within days, the world turned into a nightmare of flesh-eating Freakers. In the panic at the NERO checkpoint, Deacon St. John placed his critically injured wife, Sarah, on one of the last refugee helicopters. But there was only room for two. Faced with leaving his best friend Boozer alone in the chaos, Deacon made the impossible choice: he stayed behind. He watched the chopper fly away, believing he had saved her life. That was 730 days ago.

II. Brothers in the Shit

Two years later, Deacon and Boozer survive as drifters, running runs for camp leaders like the conspiracy theorist Mark Copeland and the slave-driving warden Ada Tucker. Their plan to ride North is shattered when Boozer is severely burned by the Rippers, a cult worshiping Freakers led by the insane Carlos. Now grounded, Deacon must navigate the tense politics of these rival camps just to keep his brother alive.

III. Ghosts of NERO

Deacon spots a black NERO helicopterâ€”the same kind that took Sarah. He tracks down NERO scientist James O'Brian, revealing a shocking truth: Sarah's camp wasn't overrun; she was moved. Fueled by a ghost of hope, Deacon begins a dangerous double life, doing dirty work for O'Brian in exchange for information on Sarah's whereabouts.

IV. Lost Lake & The Militia

Deacon finds refuge at Iron Mike's Lost Lake camp. But peace is fragile. Betrayed by Skizzo, the camp's treacherous head of security, Deacon is forced south over the Thielsen Pass. There, in the territory of the Deschutes County Militia, he finds the impossible: Sarah is alive, working as a researcher for the militia's fanatical leader, Colonel Garret.

V. The End of the World

The reunion isn't the fairytale Deacon imagined. Sarah is hardening by her mission, and Colonel Garret is descending into madness, planning a holy war. Deacon rallies his allies from Lost Lake. In an all-out assault, Deacon defeats the Colonel, dismantles the Militia, and finally claims his future with Sarah. They ride out together, no longer just surviving, but living.
